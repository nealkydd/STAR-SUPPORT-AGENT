# Astrology — Overview

Use this file to answer general questions about astrology in a calm, educational tone.
Do not make deterministic fate claims. Astrology is presented as a symbolic and reflective tool.

---

## What is astrology?

Astrology is the study of the symbolic relationships between celestial bodies — the Sun, Moon, planets, and their positions — and patterns of meaning in human life and the natural world.

In the Western Hermetic tradition, astrology is one of the foundational symbolic sciences. It operates on the principle that the cosmos and the individual are reflections of each other — as above, so below.

Astrology in this tradition is used as a tool for self-knowledge, reflection, and understanding natural cycles. It is not understood as deterministic or predictive in a mechanical sense.

---

## The birth chart

A birth chart, or natal chart, is a map of the sky at the moment and location of a person's birth. It shows the positions of the Sun, Moon, and planets across the twelve zodiac signs and twelve astrological houses.

The birth chart is commonly understood as a symbolic portrait of an individual — their tendencies, strengths, challenges, and the archetypal themes running through their life. In Stoic Qabalah, the birth chart is the foundation of the AstroQabalah reading.

---

## Key components of astrology

**Signs** — The twelve zodiac signs, each associated with an element, modality, and planetary rulership. They describe qualities of expression and character.

**Planets** — The Sun, Moon, and planets each carry symbolic meaning. Their positions by sign and house describe different dimensions of a person's nature and life themes.

**Houses** — The twelve houses of the birth chart correspond to areas of life: identity, resources, communication, home, creativity, health, relationships, transformation, philosophy, vocation, community, and the inner life.

**Aspects** — Geometric relationships between planets that describe how different energies interact — harmoniously, with tension, or with transformative potential.

---

## Astrology and the Stoic Qabalah tradition

In Stoic Qabalah, astrology is studied alongside the Tree of Life. The planets and signs are placed in correspondence with the Sephiroth and Paths, creating a unified symbolic framework.

Students learn to read their birth chart not simply as a personality profile, but as a map of their inner world — the forces at work within them, and how these relate to the great symbolic structures of the Western Hermetic tradition.
