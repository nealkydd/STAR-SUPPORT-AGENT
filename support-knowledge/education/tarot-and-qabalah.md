## What is Tarot?

Tarot is a symbolic system of 78 cards used for reflection, contemplation, and self-inquiry. The deck is divided into two main sections: the Major Arcana (22 cards) and the Minor Arcana (56 cards).

In the Western Hermetic tradition, Tarot is understood not primarily as a fortune-telling tool, but as a rich symbolic language — a visual map of human experience, inner states, and archetypal forces.

---

## The Major Arcana

The 22 Major Arcana cards depict archetypal figures and forces — The Fool, The Magician, The High Priestess, The Emperor, The Tower, The Star, and so on. Each card represents a significant quality or stage of inner development.

In Hermetic Qabalah, the 22 Major Arcana correspond directly to the 22 Paths of the Tree of Life. Each Path carries a Hebrew letter, an astrological correspondence, and a Tarot card — creating a three-way symbolic relationship that is central to Qabalistic study.

---

## The Minor Arcana

The 56 Minor Arcana are divided into four suits — Wands, Cups, Swords, and Pentacles (or Coins). Each suit corresponds to one of the four elements:

- Wands — Fire
- Cups — Water
- Swords — Air
- Pentacles — Earth

Each suit runs from Ace to Ten, followed by four Court Cards. In Qabalistic terms, the numbered cards correspond to the ten Sephiroth expressed through each element, and the Court Cards represent stages of elemental expression.

---

## How Tarot connects to Qabalah

The Hermetic Order of the Golden Dawn in the late 19th century systematised the relationship between Tarot and the Tree of Life, establishing the correspondences that underpin most Western esoteric Tarot work.

Through this framework, each Tarot card becomes a key to a specific sphere or path on the Tree of Life — and studying the Tree through the Tarot (and vice versa) deepens understanding of both systems.

---

## Tarot in the AstroQabalah deck

The AstroQabalah 92-card deck developed by Cathy Stronach extends this tradition, incorporating astrological and Qabalistic correspondences into a unified deck. The 40 Qabalah cards correspond to the major arcana and Tree of Life structure, while the 52 Astrological cards work with the signs, planets, and natural cycles.

The deck is designed as a learning and reflection tool — suitable for beginners and experienced students alike.

---

## Tarot as a reflective tool

In Stoic Qabalah, Tarot is approached as a symbolic language for self-inquiry and contemplation. A card drawn or studied is an invitation to reflect — on a quality of energy present in one's life, an aspect of one's nature, or a stage of inner development being navigated.

It is not used as a system of prediction or deterministic guidance.
