# Planetary Correspondences — Overview

Use this file to answer questions about planets and their symbolic meanings in astrology and the Qabalah tradition.
Do not make deterministic fate claims. These are symbolic correspondences, traditionally understood.

---

## The planets in Western astrology and Hermetic tradition

In Western astrology and Hermetic Qabalah, the planets are understood as symbolic forces — each associated with a distinct quality of energy, consciousness, and life theme. They are placed in correspondence with the Sephiroth of the Tree of Life, creating a unified symbolic map.

---

## Planetary meanings and Tree of Life correspondences

**The Sun**
Commonly associated with identity, vitality, purpose, and the conscious self. The Sun represents the animating principle — the core of who one is. On the Tree of Life, the Sun corresponds to Tiphareth, the sphere of beauty, harmony, and the heart of the Tree.

**The Moon**
Commonly associated with feeling, instinct, memory, and the unconscious. The Moon represents the inner world — emotional responses, cycles, and the realm of dreams. On the Tree of Life, the Moon corresponds to Yesod, the sphere of foundation and the astral realm.

**Mercury**
Commonly associated with mind, communication, perception, and the exchange of ideas. Mercury represents the capacity to think, analyse, and connect. On the Tree of Life, Mercury corresponds to Hod, the sphere of intellect and communication.

**Venus**
Commonly associated with beauty, desire, relationship, and creative force. Venus represents the power of attraction, harmony, and the appreciation of form. On the Tree of Life, Venus corresponds to Netzach, the sphere of feeling and natural force.

**Mars**
Commonly associated with will, action, courage, and assertive energy. Mars represents the drive to act, to defend, and to initiate. On the Tree of Life, Mars corresponds to Geburah, the sphere of strength and severity.

**Jupiter**
Commonly associated with expansion, wisdom, generosity, and the search for meaning. Jupiter represents the impulse to grow, explore, and understand. On the Tree of Life, Jupiter corresponds to Chesed, the sphere of mercy and grace.

**Saturn**
Commonly associated with structure, discipline, limitation, and the capacity to endure. Saturn represents the principle of form, time, and responsibility. On the Tree of Life, Saturn corresponds to Binah, the sphere of understanding and the great mother of form.

**Uranus**
Commonly associated with innovation, awakening, and the sudden disruption of established patterns. Uranus represents the principle of originality and liberation from convention.

**Neptune**
Commonly associated with imagination, spiritual longing, dissolution, and the mystical. Neptune represents the capacity to transcend ordinary boundaries and touch the numinous.

**Pluto**
Commonly associated with transformation, power, death, and regeneration. Pluto represents the deep forces of change — the removal of what no longer serves, and the emergence of new life.

---

## The classical seven and the outer planets

Traditional Hermetic Qabalah works primarily with the seven classical planets — Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, Sun, Venus, Mercury, Moon — which correspond directly to the seven lower Sephiroth of the Tree of Life. The three outer planets (Uranus, Neptune, Pluto) were discovered in the modern era and are integrated into astrological work in various ways depending on the tradition.

In AstroQabalah, planetary correspondences are explored in depth as part of the Tree of Life reading and Oracle consultation.
