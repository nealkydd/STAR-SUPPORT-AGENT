## What is Stoicism?

Stoicism is a school of philosophy founded in Athens around 300 BCE by Zeno of Citium. It was later developed by thinkers including Epictetus, Marcus Aurelius, and Seneca, whose writings remain widely studied today.

At its core, Stoicism teaches that we cannot control external events — only our own responses, judgements, and values. Virtue, reason, and living in accordance with nature are considered the foundations of a good life.

---

## Core Stoic principles

The Stoics identified four cardinal virtues:

- Wisdom — the ability to discern what is truly good
- Courage — facing difficulty with clarity and resolve
- Justice — acting rightly toward others
- Temperance — maintaining balance and self-discipline

These are understood as expressions of a single unified virtue: living according to reason.

---

## The Stoic view of nature and cosmos

Stoicism holds that the cosmos is governed by a rational principle — called the Logos — that permeates all things. Human beings, sharing in this Logos, are capable of living in harmony with the natural order.

In Stoic Qabalah, this rational principle is explored through the symbolic framework of the Tree of Life, where Stoic virtues and Hermetic wisdom are studied together as complementary paths.

---

## Stoicism and the Stoic Qabalah tradition

Stoic Qabalah draws on classical Stoic philosophy as one of its foundational strands. The Stoic emphasis on virtue, self-knowledge, inner discipline, and alignment with natural law provides a grounding counterpart to the symbolic and mystical dimensions of Qabalah and Astrology.

Students in this tradition are encouraged to study Stoicism not as abstract theory, but as a living practice — a way of approaching daily life with greater clarity, equanimity, and purpose.

---

## Recommended starting points

For those new to Stoicism, commonly recommended texts include:

- Meditations by Marcus Aurelius
- Letters from a Stoic by Seneca
- The Enchiridion by Epictetus

These texts are considered foundational and are widely available.
